Introduction

In modern manufacturing, verifying component conditions at the output stage is critical. This ensures product integrity and reveals insights across the production chain—from raw materials to external influences like personnel and machinery. Industry 4.0 raises the bar, demanding more advanced quality control methods.

Amid rapid hardware advances, computer vision has become a powerful solution. It involves acquiring, processing, and interpreting images to generate data that supports automation and smarter decision-making in industrial environments.

Motivation

As manufacturing moves toward full automation, fast and reliable quality inspection becomes crucial. Manual checks are often inconsistent, and traditional rule-based vision systems fail to catch subtle or novel defects.

Computer vision overcomes these challenges by enabling accurate, real-time defect detection. This is especially important in high-risk industries like aerospace and pharmaceuticals. By improving precision and reducing waste, it supports Industry 4.0 goals and makes automated visual inspection a key priority.

Aerospace Industry

In the aerospace industry, advanced visual analysis technologies have improved safety, maintenance, and manufacturing efficiency. By enabling early detection of structural issues, they help prevent failures and extend component life. These tools also enhance quality control on production lines and support navigation and obstacle detection in flight operations, contributing to more reliable and efficient aerospace systems.
