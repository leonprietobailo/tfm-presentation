YOLO Framework

The YOLO (You Only Look Once) framework, developed by Ultralytics, is a widely adopted and powerful tool in modern computer vision. Initially proposed as a single-stage regression approach to object detection, it has evolved up to YOLOv11 with notable gains in accuracy, speed, and usability.

A key strength is its high inference speed, making it ideal for real-time applications such as autonomous driving, surveillance, and industrial inspection. YOLO also offers a wide range of pre-trained models for tasks like detection, classification, and segmentation.

Its design abstracts complex processes like class imbalance handling, data augmentation, and model optimization, making it highly user-friendly for both researchers and developers.

Semi-supervised Learning

The semi-supervised paradigm combines a small set of labeled data with a larger set of unlabeled data to enhance learning performance. By exploiting the inherent structure in unlabeled data, it becomes highly effective when labeled data is limited or expensive, striking a balance between supervised and unsupervised learning.

This approach is applied to train segmentation models that differentiate the interior from the background of parts. The Roboflow tool is used to annotate image regions corresponding to the parts.
