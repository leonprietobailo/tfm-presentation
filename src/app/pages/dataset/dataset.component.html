Structure

The use case selected for this thesis involves the identification of manufacturing defects in impellers of submersible pumps. The dataset consists of 7.354 grayscale images with a resolution of 300 × 300 pixels. These images are categorized into two classes:

  • OK: Represents impellers that do not exhibit any manufacturing defects.
  • DEF: Denotes impellers that show defects resulting from various manufacturing errors.

The primary objective of this thesis is to analyze different strategies and solutions for developing computer vision models capable of reliably distinguishing between these two classes.

Data Cleaning and Verification

Before working with the aforementioned dataset, it is essential to perform thorough validation and preprocessing. The primary objectives are to ensure the integrity of the dataset, to prevent any data leakage between the training and testing subsets (“train” and “test”), and to apply transformations to the file metadata that may facilitate tasks such as reconstructing the original directory structure.

In this context, the following operations are carried out:

  • Duplicate Element Analysis: Potential data contamination between the training and testing sets is investigated to ensure that evaluations are conducted on data previously unseen by the model.
  • Grayscale Analysis: The dataset’s author states that the images are in grayscale, meaning each image contains only one channel instead of the standard three (RGB). Incorrect assumptions regarding this property may result in information loss or other inconsistencies during processing.
  • Unique Identifier Generation: The use of external dataset managers, such as Roboflow, may alter the original directory structure. Therefore, the initial structure is recorded in the file metadata to enable its later recovery or verification.
