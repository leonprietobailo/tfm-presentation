GradCAM

Grad-CAM is an XAI method that improves CNN interpretability by generating class-specific heatmaps, highlighting image regions that influence predictions—without altering the model or retraining.

It calculates gradients of a target class with respect to feature maps, averages them into importance weights, and applies ReLU to produce a heatmap overlaid on the image.

Grad-CAM aids in validation and debugging, ensuring models focus on meaningful regions. It fosters trust and transparency in critical applications, supporting responsible AI use.

Experiment with Pre-trained DenseNet121

Evaluation on the test set confirmed the model’s predictive performance and validated Grad-CAM as an effective tool for post-hoc interpretability. Heatmaps reliably highlighted key defect areas, aligning with regions a human inspector would focus on.

In complex cases with multiple subtle defects, activation maps appeared more dispersed, showing the model aggregated minor cues. While less intuitive, these still offer insight into the model’s reasoning and aid in debugging and refinement.

Overall, Grad-CAM enhances both transparency and reliability in industrial defect detection, especially in visually complex scenarios.
